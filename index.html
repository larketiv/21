Rules & Mechanics

// objective

Everyone plays against the dealer. Get closer to 21 than the dealer without going over. A hand exceeding 21 is a bust and the wager is lost.

// card values

Jacks, Queens, Kings, and 10s count as 10. Aces count as 1 or 11. All other cards are face value.

// gameplay

You receive two cards face up. The dealer takes one face up, one face down. Choose to Hit (draw a card) or Stand (hold your hand). Draw as many cards as you want until satisfied or until you bust.

// payouts

Beat the dealer and receive an equal payout. Dealer busts and you win. A tied total is a push and the offering returns. Blackjack pays 3 to 2.

// blackjack

An Ace paired with any 10-value card on your first two cards. Pays 3 to 2.

// split

First two cards of equal value: split into two separate hands. Second hand requires a matching offering. Split Aces receive one card each only.

// double down

After receiving your first two cards, double the offering up to the original amount. You receive exactly one more card.

// insurance

Dealer shows an Ace: wager up to half your offering that the dealer holds Blackjack. Pays 2 to 1 if correct. Lost otherwise.

// surrender

After your first two cards: forfeit half your offering and walk away from the hand immediately.

// dealer rules

Dealer must draw on 16 or below. Dealer holds on 17 or above.
